======================
Publication Template
======================


Purpose
========
This document will help you create a Publication Template in Contentserv PXP application.


Target Audience
================

* Template Planners
* Managers
* Product Owners


Prerequisites
==============

* Document templates


Overview
=========
A publication template describes the structure of a publication, e.g. a handout, as a sequence of Adobe © InDesign © document templates and is assigned to a sales channel.

The publication template references document templates (changes to document templates are thus automatically effective for the publication template used).

The page extent of a publication template results from the sum of the pages of the referenced document templates.

A publication template can refer to the same document template multiple times.

|

How-to: Create a Publication Template in Contentserv PXP Application
======================================================================


* Login into the Contentserv PXP system.

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/1.PNG
|

* Select the **MxM** catalog from the drop-down menu in the header.

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/2.PNG
|

* Scroll down to the **Template** menu and click on the **Create** button to create a new Publication Template.

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/2.PNG
|

* Click on the **Planner** icon to be able to add the document templates to the new Publication Template.

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/3.PNG
|

* Add document templates to the Publication Template Planner.

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/4.PNG
|

* Select the document templates to be added to your publication template and click on **Add**. *Note: You may add a document template more than once.*

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/5.PNG
|

* Save the changes.

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/6.PNG
|

* You may rearrange the document templates before clicking on **Save** and then **Back**.

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/7.PNG
|

* View the properties of the Publication template as you click on the **Properties** tab.

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/prop.PNG
|

* Clicking on the *Relationship* tab of Publication Template will show the document templates. (*Even if you have selected a document template multiple times, in this space, you will get to see its single representation only)

.. image:: /feature/_images/mxmimages/publicationtemplate/8.PNG
|
